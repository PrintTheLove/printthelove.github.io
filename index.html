Print the Love: Serving the World with Polaroid Pictures

Print the Love is about more than just giving away a photograph to those who have none. It is about hope, love, and encouragement. It is about uplifting others, helping them see the beauty in themselves regardless of their circumstance.

The Impact

The impact of Print the Love is felt by across people groups, ages, races, cultures and languages. To be able to print on the spot within seconds with Polaroid products opens up the doors! It serves those who normally wouldn’t be able to receive such a gift. In order to maximize impact, Print the Love hosts expeditions for those who want to particpate in the cause firsthand.

“I'll never forget the joy on the faces of each person recieveing the Love Polaroid photo. It was like joy exploding! It stays with you forever!" - Hanna Lemme, Rwanda Team Member and Board Director

The Vision

It is Print the Love's vision to see every person on earth feel loved, valued, and celebrated even amidst life’s most difficult trials; that all would have access to photographs of family, friends, and life’s most cherished memories and milestones.

The Mission

To celebrate the value and dignity of individuals in under-resourced communities through the gift of a Love Polaroid photograph.

ChildrenServed

Children in many under-resourced communities are marginalized and often not celebrated for who they are. We want to change that! Self-worth is important for emotional and social development. Very few children get school photos taken or have any record of their childhood. In addition, if a child were to be lost or taken, there would be a photograph to help find him or her.

HospitalizedEncouraged

New mothers in maternity wards around the globe rarely have access to photographs of their newborns and this lifechanging event. Others who are losing a loved one would like to cherish the last moments they have together. Even those in the hospital for more minor issues feel the value of the gift as a break in the day with warmth of the a smile. All feel encouraged that they are not forgotten in a difficult time.

Relief EffortsSupported

Many Individuals who have been through natural disasters or war have lost everything, including personal belongings and family treasures. Print the Love is able to help them rebuild emotionally by giving them new keepsakes.

FamiliesAdvocated For

Family photos have always been treasured. Children grow up fast, family members pass away. Life moves quickly and celebrating the ones we love is vital to family life.

Sustainability

As Print the Love looks to the future, we believe Print the Love can stand on its own as a cause to be supported. But we clearly see that the partnership possiblities are endless with humanitarian efforts around the world. From US based organizations to local agencies on the ground in each county, partnership and need are ever-present. With strategic partnerships like the one we have with the Polaroid Corporation, we are excited to see the impact Print the Love can make.

The Story

As a nonprofit consultant, in the summer of 2013, Ceallaigh Anderson had the privilege of traveling to Rwanda with a leadership speaking team.

While there she was able to travel to small towns and villages, schools, hospitals, churches, a prison, and engage with the rich and the poor, from politicians to the homeless. And one thing was true across the board. Everyone loved to have their picture taken. But of course it was the eagerness of the children that had the most impact on her. As soon as she took a picture, each child always wanted to look at the back of her digital camera to see themselves or their family and friends. She desperately wanted to be able to print one out and give it to them!

Thus Print the Love was born! Polaroid pictures always spread so much love and many smiles. She vowed to be back the following year, ready to print the love!

10 Days in the Land of 1000 Hills.
1 Encouraging Photographer.
1000 Photo Gifts.
Life Long Treasures of Love.

Ceallaigh travelled back to Rwanda the following year in 2014, during the 20th anniversary of the genocide with camera in tow as she had promised to herself. She raised funds through crowdfunding and brought two Polaroid cameras (Z340 and Z2300) and film. She was able to give away 1,000 Polaroid picture gifts to people who most likely have never had a printed photo of themselves - a treasure that they will likely keep for the rest of their lives. It was a simple small gift to say "You matter!" to each person, child, new mother, or family she encountered. The reactions of the people were profound! The project was a complete success!

The Vision Expands

What an amazing gift Ceallaigh recieved when she was able to witness her vision come alive through the smiles and joy of the Rwandan people as she handed out Polaroid pictures. Many people came forward, even strangers, to support the project with their donations. She wanted to see the vision and passion continue beyond Rwanda. So she assembled a team of people (including our friends at the Polaroid Corporation) around her who were passionate about growing an organization to enable many more people worldwide to feel the infectious joy of giving and recieving Love Polaroid pictures. Together, we can make great things happen!

A Gift

A gift so personal is seldom given in humanitarian efforts. We all know how much we love to take and get photos of our families, children, and friends. Even in this digital age in the U.S. where we have access to images anytime, we appreciate and cherish the photographs we get to hold in our hands. Imagine what it would be like to get a picture if you had very few or none. The influence on each person and their families is significant.

The Need

Our greatest need is resources - funding for travel costs and Polaroid equipment (at a very reduced rate from our friends at the Polaroid Corporation). To stay in line with our mission to celebrate all people groups and serve those in under-resourced communities, we strategically chose travel locations in 3 different continents - South America, Asia, and Africa. All are on the list of least developed and most impoverished in the world. Strategically we will travel to remote areas where electricity and especially photographs are scarce.
